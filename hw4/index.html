Overview

We implemented a real-time simulation of cloth using a mass and spring based system. Firstly, we built the point-mass data structure to discretely represent the cloth, define and apply three physical constraints (structual, shearing and bending) on the masses, and apply numerical integration to simulate the way cloth moves over time. Secondly, we implemented collisions of the cloth with other objects as well as self-collisions to prevent cloth clipping. Finally, we implemented multiple shaders (Diffuse shader, Blinn-Phong shader, Texture shader, Bumping shader, Displacement Shader and Mirror shader) using GLSL.

Part 2: Simulation via Numerical Integration

Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

ks is the spring constant, and it measures the stiffness of a spring, affecting the force applied on each point mass. When ks is small, the cloth shows more curvature on the top. When ks is large, the cloth seems to be more stiff. However, when ks is too large, the cloth behaves strangely and never reaches a resting state (shown in the bottom right image).

What about for density?

Density measures the mass of the cloth per unit area. Small density indicates less force, which makes the cloth look lighter as the curvature on the top is less significant. As density increases, the external forces have greater effect on the cloth, making greater curvature. When density is 1000 g/cm^2, the cloth never reaches a resting state.

What about for damping?

Damping helps simulate the energy loss during the process of the cloth falling down. When damping is 0%, the cloth keeps oscilating back and forth. As we increase the damping value, the cloth moves slower and slower, finally reaching its resting state.

    Explain in your own words what is a shader program and how vertex and fragment shaders work together to create lighting and material effects.

  • A shader program is a type of computer program used in computer graphics to determine the rendering of objects in a 3D scene. It operates on the GPU and is responsible for calculating the color, texture, lighting, and other visual properties of each pixel on the screen.
  • The vertex shader handles geometric transformations which determines the vertex position and prepares data for the fragment shader, while the fragment shader computes the final appearance of each pixel based on lighting, textures, and other factors.

    • Explain the Blinn-Phong shading model in your own words. Show a screenshot of your Blinn-Phong shader outputting only the ambient component, a screen shot only outputting the diffuse component, a screen shot only outputting the specular component, and one using the entire Blinn-Phong model.

  • Ambient Component: The light appears everywhere in the scene, regardless of the direct illumination which prevents the complete darkness.
  • Diffuse Component: The light is scattered equally in all directions upon hitting a surface. It is responsible for the color of an object when it is illuminated by direct light sources.
  • Specular Component: Specular highlights are bright, shiny spots on a surface that occur when light reflects off it in a concentrated manner.
  • The Blinn-Phong shading model combines the three components above to compute the final color of each pixel on a surface, resulting in a realistic representation of how light interacts with objects in a 3D scene.

    In bump shading, we create the illusion of "bumps" by changing the normal vectors at each vertex. We estimate the new normal of each vertex in local space with [dU, dV, 1] and convert it into the model space by left multiplying the TBN matrix. While in displacement shading, not only do we modify the normals, but also we update the position of vertices to reflect the height map. Comparing the two rendering results above, we can see that both shaders show the bumpy feature on the object. However, since displacement shading change each vertex position while bump shading does not, we observe that the sphere with displacement shading is rugged on the top, while the one with bump shading is smooth on its contour.
